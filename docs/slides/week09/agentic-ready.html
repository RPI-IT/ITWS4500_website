Two Types of AI

Understanding the difference matters for your career

Generative AI: The Content Creator

What it does: Creates new content based on patterns it learned

Examples:

  • ChatGPT writing an email
  • DALL-E creating an image
  • Copilot suggesting code
  • Claude summarizing a document

Key characteristic:

You ask → It answers

One input, one output

You remain in control of every step

Agentic AI: The Autonomous Worker

What it does: Plans, decides, takes actions, and adapts

Examples:

  • AI that researches competitors and writes a report
  • AI that books your travel across multiple sites
  • AI that monitors inventory and reorders supplies
  • AI that debugs code and fixes it autonomously

Key characteristic:

You set a goal → It figures out how

Multiple steps, decisions along the way

Works semi-autonomously

The Key Difference

Generative AI is a tool. Agentic AI is a collaborator.

The Empowered Consultant

A new model for knowledge work

What Is an Empowered Consultant?

Someone who can:

  • Understand business problems deeply
  • Direct AI systems to gather information and create outputs
  • Evaluate and refine AI-generated work
  • Make judgment calls that require human insight

You don’t need to code everything yourself. You need to manage AI that can code for you.

The Rise of “Vibe Coding”

Vibe coding: Describing what you want in plain language and having AI build it

Before:

“I need to learn React, CSS, database design, API development, deployment…”

Months of learning before building anything

Now:

“Build me a dashboard that shows sales by region with filters for date range and product category”

Working prototype in hours

The catch: You still need to know enough to evaluate whether it’s good.

What “Managing AI” Looks Like

  1. Framing the problem — AI can’t solve problems you can’t articulate
  2. Breaking down complex tasks — Knowing what steps are needed
  3. Providing context — AI works better with good information
  4. Quality control — Catching errors, biases, and gaps
  5. Iterating — Refining outputs through feedback

Important

The person who can do this well is extremely productive.

Rethinking Education

How we’re preparing students differently

The Old Model: Build Up Slowly

Traditional approach:

Problem: Years of “building blocks” before doing anything meaningful

The New Model: Skip the Middle

AI lets students focus on:

  • Foundations — Understanding why and what, not just how
  • High-impact contexts — Real projects, real stakeholders, real decisions

What We Mean by “Skip the Middle”

The Middle (AI can help):

  • Boilerplate code
  • Syntax memorization
  • Routine debugging
  • Standard implementations
  • Documentation formatting

The Ends (humans must own):

  • Problem understanding
  • Architectural decisions
  • User needs
  • Quality judgment
  • Ethical considerations

Students spend time on thinking, not typing.

Skills for the AI Economy

Technical Fluency (Not Expertise)

You don’t need to be a computer scientist. You need to:

  • Understand what AI can and cannot do
  • Know how to prompt effectively
  • Recognize good vs. bad AI output
  • Understand basic data concepts

Think: “Fluent tourist” not “native speaker”

Strategic Thinking

AI handles execution. Humans handle:

  • Problem definition — What are we actually trying to solve?
  • Trade-off analysis — What are we optimizing for?
  • Ethical judgment — Should we do this?
  • Stakeholder management — Who is affected?

Domain Expertise

AI is general. Value comes from specificity:

  • Deep knowledge of an industry
  • Understanding of customer needs
  • Regulatory and compliance awareness
  • Organizational context

Note

The formula: Domain Expertise + AI Fluency = Competitive Advantage

The Skills That Are Hard to Automate

Human advantages:

  • Building trust and relationships

  • Creative problem framing
  • Ethical reasoning
  • Cross-functional collaboration

AI advantages:

  • Processing large amounts of information
  • Consistency at scale
  • Pattern recognition

  • Never getting tired
